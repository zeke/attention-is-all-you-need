# Transformers & Attention Resources

## Knowledge

- [Paper: "Attention Is All You Need" — Vaswani et al., 2017 (arXiv:1706.03762)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762)
  The original transformer paper. Use for: the definitive description of the architecture, multi-head attention, positional encoding, encoder-decoder structure.

- [Paper: "Neural Machine Translation by Jointly Learning to Align and Translate" — Bahdanau et al., 2015 (arXiv:1409.0473)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1409.0473)
  The paper that introduced attention as a concept (before transformers). Use for: understanding where attention came from and the problem it solved in RNN-based seq2seq models.

- [Blog: "The Illustrated Transformer" — Jay Alammar (2018)](https://jalammar.github.io/illustrated-transformer/)
  The best visual explainer of the transformer architecture. Use for: understanding encoder/decoder, Q/K/V, multi-head attention with diagrams. Extremely high trust.

- [Blog: "The Illustrated GPT-2" — Jay Alammar (2019)](https://jalammar.github.io/illustrated-gpt2/)
  Extends the transformer explainer to the decoder-only GPT architecture. Use for: understanding how the transformer became an autoregressive language model.

- [Video: "Let's build GPT: from scratch, in code, slowly" — Andrej Karpathy (2023)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kCc8FmEb1nY)
  3.5-hour walkthrough building a GPT from scratch in PyTorch. Even without running the code, the conceptual narration is exceptional. Use for: deeply grounding any concept in first-principles.

- [Video: "Intro to Large Language Models" — Andrej Karpathy (2023)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zjkBMFhNj_g)
  1-hour accessible overview of LLMs, training, RLHF, and emergent capabilities. Use for: the bridge from transformer to modern LLM.

- [Paper: "An Image is Worth 16x16 Words: Transformers for Image Recognition at Scale" — Dosovitskiy et al., 2020 (arXiv:2010.11929)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2010.11929)
  The Vision Transformer (ViT) paper. Use for: explaining how the transformer architecture transferred to computer vision.

- [Blog: "AlphaFold 2 Explained" — DeepMind (2021)](https://www.deepmind.com/blog/alphafold-a-solution-to-a-50-year-old-grand-challenge-in-biology)
  Use for: the protein-folding breakthrough as a case study of transformers applied outside NLP.

- [Paper: "Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models" — Kaplan et al., 2020 (arXiv:2001.08361)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2001.08361)
  The foundational scaling laws paper. Use for: explaining why bigger models trained on more data reliably get better, and why this drove the LLM race.

- [Blog: "A Survey of Large Language Models" — Wayne Xin Zhao et al. (2023)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.18223)
  Comprehensive survey of the LLM landscape. Use for: overview of model families, training approaches, RLHF, emergent capabilities.

- [Video: "Attention in transformers, visually explained" — 3Blue1Brown (2024)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eMlx5fFNoYc)
  Visual walkthrough of attention mechanics from the Grant Sanderson / 3Blue1Brown neural network series. Use for: geometric intuition for what attention is doing.

## Wisdom (Communities)

- [r/MachineLearning](https://reddit.com/r/MachineLearning)
  Practitioner-level subreddit. Use for: staying current on new architectures, paper discussions.

- [Hacker News — "Ask HN" and "Show HN"](https://news.ycombinator.com)
  High-signal discussions when major papers drop. Search for paper titles or author names.

- [Alignment Forum / LessWrong](https://www.lesswrong.com)
  Deep technical writing on LLM capabilities, interpretability, and scaling. Use for: understanding emergent capabilities debates and AI safety context.
