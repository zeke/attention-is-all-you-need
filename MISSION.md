# Mission: Transformers and the Attention Mechanism

## Why

Zeke has been working in the AI space since 2021, designing product surfaces and interfaces for AI models at Cloudflare. He can use models fluently but wants to understand the architecture beneath them well enough to explain the full lineage — from n-grams through RNNs to the transformer and the modern LLM era — clearly and accurately to other people.

## Success looks like

- Can explain, without notes, what "attention" means in "Attention Is All You Need" and why it was a breakthrough
- Can trace the historical arc from n-gram language models to transformers to GPT-4 / Claude in a way a non-ML engineer would follow
- Can explain what the transformer made possible beyond NLP: vision, protein folding, code, multimodal models
- Can describe where the field is heading now (MoE, SSMs, scaling laws, agents) and why the transformer is still central
- Can read the original "Attention Is All You Need" paper and understand its structure and claims

## Constraints

- No interest in implementing ML models from scratch
- Intuition and analogy over equations; code-style pseudocode is welcome
- Learning iteratively, one lesson at a time, with debriefs between lessons
- Lessons should be completable in 10-15 minutes

## Out of scope

- Hands-on PyTorch / JAX implementation
- Detailed backpropagation or gradient descent mechanics
- Hyperparameter tuning or MLOps
